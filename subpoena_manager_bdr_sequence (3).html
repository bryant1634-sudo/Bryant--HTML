    Sequence Strategy

    How each phase of the sequence is designed to build on the last
    1 Week 1 · Days 1–7
    Educate on the Problem
    Open with operational pain — manual intake, rekeying, routing gaps. Don't lead with features. The goal is to get the prospect to recognize their current process as a problem worth fixing. Use the first email to name the chaos; use the call to get them talking about it.
    Pain framing Education Blog / LP
    2 Week 2 · Days 8–17
    Build Credibility & Relevance
    Shift from pain to risk and platform value. Introduce visibility gaps, cross-department handoff failures, and the connection to downstream eDiscovery. Reference their specific role's pain — Legal Ops cares about ROI, GC cares about defensibility, paralegals care about chasing.
    Risk frame Platform Solution brief
    3 Week 3–4 · Days 19–25
    AI Confidence + Close Loop
    Use the AI email to address the "speed vs. control" concern proactively — legal buyers want AI that is governed. The breakup email has the highest reply rate. Keep it short, re-state the two or three pain points in plain language, and lower the barrier to respond.
    Governed AI Breakup Exec brief

    Do's & Don'ts

    What separates a response from a delete
    ✓ Do This
    • Name their specific role's pain — Legal Ops cares about ROI, GC cares about defensibility
    • Ask process questions before pitching — "How are you managing subpoena routing today?"
    • Use the 90-minute stat early — it lands fast with anyone managing volume
    • Make the AI governance boundary explicit — legal buyers are risk-averse, not anti-AI
    • Connect Subpoena Manager to downstream eDiscovery — that's the platform advantage
    • Keep subject lines short and problem-oriented — not feature-oriented
    • Send the breakup email — it has the highest reply rate in the sequence
    • Personalize the trigger note line on call follow-ups with their specific stack or workflow
    ✗ Don't Do This
    • Lead with "governed autonomous AI" in the subject line — start with the problem
    • Mention AgentMode as if it is broadly available today — it is the strategic bridge
    • Say "the AI makes decisions" — it handles mechanics, humans control judgment
    • Skip the phone call touchpoints — email alone won't set this meeting
    • Send a wall of text — legal buyers skim, every email should have one clear ask
    • Use generic openers like "I hope this finds you well" or "Just circling back"
    • Over-explain the product before confirming the pain exists — qualify first
    • Forget to reference what the prospect already mentioned if you have prior context
